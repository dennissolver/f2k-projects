## 1. The positioning thesis (the reframe)

F2K should stop positioning as "the enriched estate-**website** provider" and position as:

> **The modular-delivery + project-coordination + sales-platform layer that turns a land
> developer's raw lots into sellable, buildable, affordable homes.**

- The **enriched platform** (estate pages + interactive availability + the ROI portal: agent
  attribution, waitlist→qualification funnel, masked availability, voice, funnel dashboard) is the
  **visible wedge** — the thing prospects demonstrably don't have (they run throwaway per-project
  brochure sites).
- The **modular H&L packages** (the WABI/EMU/Joey/Koala catalogue + supply coordination + H&L
  costing) are the recurring **cash engine** — per-home margin across a developer's pipeline.
- **Project coordination** (the land→build→settle orchestration Uwe already runs) is the glue.
- **Seafields / Wavecrest are the live proof** — F2K already does this entire stack on its own
  estates. This isn't a new-capability pitch; it's productising Seafields and pointing it at
  developers who have lots but no build/sell machine.

**Direction correction:** the strong arrow is F2K's model going **into developers' projects** (their
lots are the canvas), NOT bringing a metro developer **into** regional Seafields (geographic/product
mismatch). Keep capital/JV cross-pollination as upside, not the lead.

## 3. The value ladder (enter without overcommitting)
1. **Platform wedge** — co-branded/white-label enriched site + ROI portal for ONE project. Low
   commitment, solves the per-project-brochure pain, embeds F2K in sell-through, proves the tech.
2. **Modular H&L attach** — on greenfield lot sales, F2K modular H&L packages: lots → turnkey
   affordable homes, faster settlement, controlled cost. Serves the developer's ethos; F2K's cash
   engine.
3. **Coordination** — F2K orchestrates land→build→settle for those packages (productised, not
   bespoke).
4. **JV / co-development** — optionality only, where land/capital align.

## 5. Guardrails (so this serves the North Star, not swallows it)
- **Bandwidth / North Star:** a 5,000-lot developer (or a deal-architect) can re-chain the operator
  to the tireless middle as a bespoke sub. Defense: sell the **productised** platform + **standard**
  modular catalogue — repeatable across many developers, anchor tenant ≠ in-house team. If it can
  only be delivered bespoke per project, it's the wrong shape.
- **Modular-capacity honesty:** F2K is a modular **solutions integrator + H&L packager + platform**,
  not a mega-factory. Scaling to a big pipeline = scaling supply partnerships (MWA / Modular WA /
  Unison), not pretending to own the factory.
- **Platform isn't multi-tenant/white-label yet** — Tier-1 (two-level tenancy, theming, metering) is
  unbuilt. **Validate before building** (Gate-1 "I'd put my projects on this" → Gate-2 GO). The
  Phase-0 generic estate spine (`estates`/`units`/…) is a head-start, not the finished platform.
- **Small-vs-big credibility:** lead with the **live demo** (Seafields/Branscombe), not the org chart
  — the platform + a proven estate is the equalizer; they have neither.
- **A LinkedIn profile is a lead, not ground truth.** Build a *conversation* on it, not a plan.
- **Don't let it sprawl** — validate the thesis through the ONE highest-leverage node (Andrew) before
  working the whole board.
